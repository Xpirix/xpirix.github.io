PhD Research · 4/6

Key Findings

1 · Rapid Unplanned Urbanisation

Population grew from ~1.5M (1993) to 2.6M (2022). Uncontrolled expansion into risk zones, loss of wetlands and green spaces reducing natural drainage capacity.

2 · Fragile Institutional Framework

Responsibilities fragmented across ministries, communes and operators. Lack of coordination, unstable financial resources and difficulties collecting sanitation fees.

3 · Dysfunctional Sanitation Networks

445 km of network: only 34% buried. Obsolete wastewater system with low connection rate & direct discharges. Canals chronically blocked by solid waste, worsening flooding.

4 · January 2022 Flooding Impacts

4,500+ buildings damaged · ~58,000 people affected · 337 km of roads submerged — illustrating the urgent need for integrated water governance.

PhD Research · 6/6

Conclusions & Recommendations

Hypotheses — Validation
✅ Increasing development of flood & landslide risk zones
✅ Coordination failures between water management actors
✅ Need for an integrated vision across all stakeholders
⚠️ Curative rather than preventive management (partially confirmed)
Recommendations
  • Strengthen the institutional framework and inter-actor coordination
  • Ensure sustainable financing for the sanitation sector
  • Develop locally adapted technical solutions for stormwater collection & wastewater treatment
  • Implement an early warning system for flood & landslide risks
  • Build a centralised geoportal for data management & decision support → Geosanitation

One unified ecosystem: desktop analysis → QGIS Server → web portal. Zero data format translation.
